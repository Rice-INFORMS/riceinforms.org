What is Operations Research?

Operations research (OR) is a multidisciplinary field that applies advanced analytical methods to help make better decisions. By using techniques from mathematics, statistics, computer science, and economics, OR aims to provide a rational basis for decision-making by seeking to understand and structure complex situations and to predict system behavior. It involves the development and application of models and algorithms to solve problems in various domains such as logistics, supply chain management, finance, healthcare, and transportation. The goal is to optimize performance, improve efficiency, and reduce costs.

At the core of operations research are methods such as linear programming, queuing theory, simulation, and network analysis. These techniques allow OR professionals to analyze data, model systems, and evaluate potential solutions to determine the most effective course of action. For example, OR can be used to optimize scheduling in manufacturing processes, design efficient delivery routes for logistics companies, or manage inventory levels in retail. By providing a systematic and quantitative approach to problem-solving, operations research helps organizations make informed, data-driven decisions and achieve operational excellence.
